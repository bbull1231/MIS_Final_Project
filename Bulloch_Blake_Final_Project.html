Summary/My Insights¶

When I pulled all the data and ran correlations, pivot tables, and graphs it was hard to tell if there were actualy relationships or patterns at all until I used the mean count. When I used the mean count there were some patterns such as the longer the average of duration, the better the average imdb scores. I think there is a lot of factors determining what will give you a high score and what will not. There was no definite correlation showing what may increase the score of a movie besides possibly the duration.

Conclusion Findings¶

Based on my findings I would not recommend using Regression analysis because of the low variance at an 0.2.</br> When comparing the two cluster analysis they seem to be very similiar. Something I noticed that was a little difference, was the Agglomerative clustering
sizes seemed to be bigger in their profiles of each cluster and the K-Mean clustering profiles were a little smaller in profile size.

In the past, I would personally use a K-Means because I personally think it is easier to read and understand. However, this isn't the case here.

KNN in classification had the highest variance at an 0.5 and this would be my recommendation.
